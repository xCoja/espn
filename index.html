Power Rankings: The Top 10 NBA 2K26 PC Players Right Now

By Brian Windhorst & ESPN eSports Staff
Updated: Feb 20, 2026, 9:14 AM ET
The PC meta for NBA 2K26 has evolved, and a new undisputed king has claimed the throne. (ESPN Illustration)

With the NBA 2K26 season in full swing, the PC lobbies have turned into an absolute bloodbath. We've seen meta builds rise and fall, patch notes destroy careers, and controller players dominate the landscape—until now. Our panel of ESPN eSports analysts have scoured the leaderboards, reviewed the tape, and put together the definitive Top 10 PC players on the hardwood right now.

1
Mack (KEYBOARD PLAYER)
Defying all logic, biomechanics, and the natural laws of gaming, Mack takes the #1 spot. While the rest of the league relies on custom controllers with aim assist and precise analog movement, this absolute unit is out here greening 35-foot step-backs with WASD and a spacebar. It's a level of mechanical precision that has terrified the 2K community. Unprecedented. Undisputed.
2
IsoGodxx
The name speaks for itself. IsoGodxx will dribble the air out of the ball for exactly 23 seconds, ignore three wide-open teammates in the corner, and confidently green a 100% smothered fadeaway over two 7-footers. It's infuriating to watch, but you simply cannot argue with the results.
3
PassOrIDontPlayD
A psychological terrorist on the virtual court. If he doesn't get a touch on the first offensive possession, he will literally camp out of bounds for the rest of the quarter. Yet, when fully engaged, he's a two-way demon who completely locks down the opposing team's best player.
4
SheLuvRelly
Spend 5 minutes in a Rec lobby and you'll run into someone with a "SheLuv" tag, but Relly is the only one who backs it up. A flashy, high-IQ point guard who spends more time doing pre-game emotes than warming up, yet currently leads the PC circuit in assists and steals.
5
SkillIssue
Running a 6'9" demigod build that breaks every positional rule in the game, SkillIssue drops 40 points a night just to open his mic in the post-game lobby and yell his own username. He's arguably the most hated player in the Top 10, but the talent is undeniable.
6
JustDrawTheOut
The undisputed king of intercepting passing lanes. It feels like he has a 12-foot wingspan. You can throw a cross-court pass to a wide open man, and JustDrawTheOut will somehow teleport from the paint to snag it out of mid-air. An absolute defensive nightmare.
7
iBetrash
A master of reverse psychology. Opponents see the username and assume an easy win, right up until he drops a flawless 30-point triple-double. His pump-fake game belongs in a museum.
8
LockDimer
The pure playmaker everyone wants on their team but no one deserves. LockDimer has zero interest in scoring. He just wants to throw full-court alley-oops and rack up Hall of Fame Dimer badges. A breath of fresh air in a highly selfish meta.
9
YRN_Nate
Unpredictable, chaotic, and entirely unguardable. YRN_Nate plays the game like it's a 1v5. He takes terrible shots, plays reckless defense, and somehow, miraculously, it translates to an 85% win rate. Scouting reports on him are utterly useless.
10
coja
Rounding out the top 10 is coja. Does he have the highest win percentage on the list? Absolutely not. Does he blame the server latency every time he misses a "slightly-early" release? Consistently. But when the lights are brightest and the Pro-Am squad needs a clutch bucket, coja is there to hit the game-tying corner three... right before his internet inevitably drops. A true PC legend in his own chaotic way.
