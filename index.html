Power Rankings: The Top 10 NBA 2K26 PC Players Right Now

By Brian Windhorst & ESPN eSports Staff
Updated: Feb 20, 2026, 9:14 AM ET
The PC meta for NBA 2K26 has evolved, and a new undisputed king has claimed the throne. (ESPN Illustration)

With the NBA 2K26 season in full swing, the PC lobbies have turned into an absolute bloodbath. We've seen meta builds rise and fall, patch notes destroy careers, and controller players dominate the landscape. Our panel of ESPN eSports analysts have scoured the leaderboards to put together the definitive Top 10 PC players on the hardwood right now.

1
Mack (KEYBOARD PLAYER)
Defying all logic, Mack remains the undisputed #1, even though he just missed four wide-open shots in the Rec. It’s becoming clear he has no eyes and desperately needs a sponsorship from glasses.com. Yet, somehow, this absolute unit still dominates on a keyboard. He is so transcendent that he can play on any build—6'10", 6'6", 6'11" sometimes on 7'0 even tho he is trash but still look untouchable, greening impossible left side AND RIGHT SIDE fades with 2 on a numpad. Unprecedented.
2
Mysterio
The greatest point guard to ever touch the game. Period. Mysterio operates on a level of basketball IQ that makes the rest of the league look like they’re playing in slow motion. His handles are a work of art, and his ability to dictate the flow of the game is legendary. We are witnessing a once-in-a-generation talent who glazes the court with pure skill. Sometimes like to shoot 100% smothered but it's fine its mysterio that's his signature.
3
Crystal
Don't let the "little sister" title fool you; Mysterio’s sibling is the most dominant force in the paint. Crystal is officially the best board snatcher in NBA 2K26. She is out-rebounding Mack, Mysterio, and coja combined. If the ball is in the air, it’s already hers. A rebounding savant that has completely shifted the interior meta.
4
SheLuvRelly
A flashy, high-IQ point guard who spends more time doing pre-game emotes than warming up, yet currently leads the PC circuit in assists and steals.
5
SkillIssue
Running a 6'9" demigod build, SkillIssue drops 40 points a night just to open his mic in the post-game lobby and yell his own username. The talent is undeniable, even if the attitude isn't.
6
JustDrawTheOut
The undisputed king of intercepting passing lanes. It feels like he has a 12-foot wingspan, teleporting from the paint to snag cross-court passes out of mid-air.
7
iBetrash
A master of reverse psychology. Opponents assume an easy win right up until he drops a flawless 30-point triple-double. His pump-fake game belongs in a museum.
8
LockDimer
The pure playmaker everyone wants on their team. LockDimer has zero interest in scoring, focusing entirely on full-court alley-oops and Hall of Fame Dimer badges.
9
YRN_Nate
Unpredictable and entirely unguardable. YRN_Nate plays the game like it's a 1v5, taking terrible shots that somehow translate to an 85% win rate.
10
coja
Rounding out the top 10 is coja, who is undeniably the worst player alive. While he can occasionally hit a right-side fadeaway 3, that doesn't make him good. He’s still chucking up bricks on his new PG build and selling his teammates every single game. A complete liability who is lucky the rankings stopped at ten.
